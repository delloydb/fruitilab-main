Myth Busters

Separating fact from fiction

Juicing removes insoluble fiber but retains soluble fiber. For example, a glass of orange juice still contains about 0.5g of soluble fiber per cup.

Whole fruits contain natural sugars packaged with fiber, water, and nutrients. The problem arises when juicing removes fiber, concentrating sugars.
